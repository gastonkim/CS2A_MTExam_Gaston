THE CALLE CRISOLOGO (Vigan City, Ilocos Sur)

Calle Crisologo is a well-preserved heritage street in Vigan City that showcases the beauty of Spanish colonial architecture. Its cobblestone road, antique houses, and horse-drawn kalesa make it feel like stepping back in time. Today, it stands as one of the Philippines’ most famous cultural treasures and a favorite spot for travelers.

BALUARTE ZOO (Vigan City, Ilocos Sur)

Baluarte Zoo is one of the top attractions in Vigan City, offering close encounters with a wide variety of animals. The zoo is owned by former governor Chavit Singson and features tigers, reptiles, birds, and safari-inspired shows. It is a family-friendly destination where visitors can enjoy both wildlife and panoramic views of Vigan.

STA MARIA CHURCH (Santa Maria, Ilocos Sur)

The Sta. Maria Church, officially known as the Church of the Assumption, is one of the four Baroque Churches of the Philippines recognized by UNESCO. Built in 1765, it stands on top of a hill and is famous for its massive brick structure and fortress-like design. The church is a symbol of faith and heritage, drawing pilgrims and tourists alike.
